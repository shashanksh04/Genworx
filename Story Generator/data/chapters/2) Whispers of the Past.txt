### Arrival in the Town of Blackthorn  

Emily Baxter stepped off the creaking bus and exhaled sharply, the cold night air biting at her skin. The town of Blackthorn was nestled deep in the woods, its streets lined with weathered buildings that had long since lost their luster. The air was thick with an eerie stillness, broken only by the occasional caw of a distant raven. As a paranormal investigator, she had seen her fair share of haunted locations, but there was something about this place that set it apart. Whispers of the mansion’s dark past had reached her through the usual channels—grainy photos, obscure journal entries, and the occasional terrified testimonial from locals who refused to speak of it. Whatever secrets the mansion held, they were far from being forgotten.  

Emily adjusted her coat and scanned the town, her sharp eyes taking in the wary glances of the few residents who had emerged from their homes. There was an unspoken tension in the air, a collective unease that clung to the streets like a lingering shadow. She had no intention of lingering in this town for long, but she needed answers, and the mansion was the only lead she had.  

Her destination was clear: Blackthorn Manor, the decaying estate that had become a local legend. Even from a distance, the mansion loomed like a specter from another time, its once-proud façade now a crumbling monument to forgotten sins. Some claimed it had been abandoned after a series of tragic deaths, while others believed it had been cursed by the very forces that had once dwelled within its walls. Emily had no illusions about the dangers that awaited her, but she had spent years chasing the unknown, and she was not one to back down from a mystery.  

As she made her way toward the mansion, the silence around her deepened, as though the very air was holding its breath. Something about this place felt wrong—off-kilter in a way that defied logic. And as she neared the mansion’s entrance, she felt it: a presence watching her, waiting.

### The Girl with the Unseen Burden  

Inside the small, dimly lit house on the outskirts of Blackthorn, Sarah Mills sat on the edge of her bed, staring at the ceiling as if it might offer her some answers. The room was sparse—barely furnished, save for a worn-out armchair and a single lamp that flickered in the darkness. Her mother had long since left, and the house had become a prison of silence and shadows, a place where the only company Sarah had was the lingering weight of the visions that plagued her.  

She had no idea what they meant—only that they had started after the accident, the one that had taken her best friend’s life. At first, it had been dreams, fleeting and fragmented, like half-remembered fragments of a nightmare. But over time, the dreams had grown more vivid, more insistent. Now, she could feel them even when her eyes were open—glimpses of a mansion that was never far from her mind, a place that seemed to call to her from the depths of her subconscious. The mansion was never the same in each vision; sometimes it was a crumbling ruin, other times it shimmered with an unnatural light. But always, there was the shadow—tall, indistinct, and watching her.  

Sarah hated the visions. They left her exhausted, her head throbbing with a pain that no medication could soothe. She had tried to ignore them, to push them from her mind, but the more she resisted, the more they invaded her. She had told no one about them, not even the few friends she had left. She was afraid of what they might think—afraid that they would see her as crazy, or worse, as something broken.  

Still, there was a strange comfort in the visions, a pull that she could not explain. They felt... familiar. As if the mansion was not a stranger to her, but something she had once known.  

The door creaked open behind her.  

Sarah turned, her heart pounding in her chest. A woman stood in the doorway, her presence cutting through the darkness like a blade. Her eyes were sharp, assessing, and there was something in her stance that told Sarah this was not someone to be feared, but someone who had seen things she had not.  

“You’re Sarah Mills, aren’t you?” the woman asked, her voice calm but firm.  

Sarah hesitated, then nodded slowly.  

The woman stepped closer, her expression unreadable. “You’ve seen things, haven’t you?”  

Sarah swallowed hard. “Who are you?”  

“Emily Baxter. I’m here because of the mansion.”  

Sarah’s breath caught in her throat. The mansion.  

And in the back of her mind, the shadow stirred once more.

### A Shared Purpose  

Sarah hesitated for a moment, the weight of the woman’s words settling over her like a shroud. The mansion. The very thought of it sent a shiver down her spine, but something in Emily’s presence—her unwavering certainty, the way she carried herself with the confidence of someone who had spent years chasing the unknown—gave her a strange sense of reassurance.  

“I… I don’t know what you mean,” Sarah said, her voice barely above a whisper.  

Emily studied her for a moment, then stepped fully into the room. “You do. You’ve seen it. Maybe not in the way I have, but you’ve seen something. That’s why you’re here, Sarah. That’s why you’re afraid.”

Emily didn’t press her. Instead, she moved to sit on the edge of the bed, her gaze steady. “You’re not the first person I’ve met who’s been drawn to that place. People come here with questions, with fear. But eventually, they all end up facing the same thing.”  

Sarah’s eyes flickered toward her, uncertainty warring with something else—something deeper, something she wasn’t ready to name. “What do you mean?”  

Emily leaned forward slightly, her voice dropping to a near whisper. “The mansion isn’t just a place. It’s a presence. Something that’s been waiting for a long time.”  

Sarah’s breath hitched. She wanted to look away, to pretend she wasn’t hearing this, but the words had already taken root in her mind. The shadow. The mansion. The thing that had been watching her from the beginning.  

She looked back at Emily, her eyes wide. “You think I… I have a connection to it?”  

Emily didn’t answer immediately. Instead, she reached out, her hand hovering just inches from Sarah’s. “I think you’re going to find out soon enough.”  

A strange feeling settled in Sarah’s chest—something between fear and curiosity. She had spent so much of her life running from the visions, from the things she couldn’t explain. But now, standing here with Emily, she felt something shift inside her. Not just curiosity, but a quiet certainty that maybe, just maybe, this was the answer she had been looking for.  

She took a deep breath and nodded slowly. “Then I guess I have no choice but to follow you.”  

Emily gave her a small, knowing smile. “Good. Because this isn’t just about you anymore.”  

Sarah swallowed hard, but she didn’t look away. The mansion had always been waiting. Now, it seemed, the waiting was over.

### Into the Shadows

A sudden gust of wind swept through the room, though no windows were open. The papers on the desk fluttered, and the shadows at the edges of the room seemed to shift, coalescing into something that wasn’t quite human.  

Sarah took a step back, her heart pounding.  

Emily’s hand shot out, gripping Sarah’s wrist. “We need to go,” she said, her voice firm.  

But something else was happening now. The mansion was speaking to them. And it wasn’t about to let them leave without an answer.

### Echoes of the Past

### The Mirror’s Awakening  

The shadow lunged forward, but before it could reach them, Emily grabbed Sarah’s hand and pulled her toward the door. The moment they stepped into the hallway, the air shifted again, thick with an unseen force. The mansion was no longer just watching them—it was reacting to them, as if they had awakened something it had long been trying to keep hidden.  

Emily didn’t let go of Sarah’s hand as they ran, their footsteps echoing through the empty halls. The mansion was alive in a way that defied explanation, its walls pulsing with an energy that sent a shiver through Sarah’s spine.  

They reached the grand foyer again, and as Emily turned to lead them further into the mansion, a sudden gust of wind swept through the room. The chandelier above them flickered violently, its crystals casting jagged shadows across the floor.  

Then, the mirror.

It was taller than a man, its surface smooth and impossibly dark, as if it were not glass at all but something else entirely—something that had swallowed the light and left nothing behind.  

Sarah’s breath caught in her throat as she stared at it. The mirror was not just reflecting the room—it was *watching* them.  

Emily’s grip on her hand tightened. “Don’t look into it,” she said, her voice low. “It doesn’t want us to.”  

But Sarah couldn’t look away. The mirror’s surface was shifting, its darkness rippling like liquid. And within that darkness, something was moving—something ancient, something that had been waiting for this moment.

“*You have come back to us.*”

Emily pulled her back, her face pale. “We need to leave. Now.”

The air thickened, and the shadows in the room surged forward, forming into shapes that twisted and writhed, as though they were trying to reach them. The mansion was no longer just a house. It was a prison, and they had awakened something that had been locked away for far too long.  

Emily grabbed Sarah’s hand again, pulling her toward the hallway. “We have to go!”  

But the mirror’s voice was louder now, more insistent.  

“*You cannot leave.*”  

Sarah felt her body being pulled toward the mirror, as if an invisible force was dragging her toward it. She tried to resist, but her legs refused to move. Emily’s grip was firm, but it wasn’t enough.  

Then, a sudden, blinding light erupted from the mirror.  

A surge of energy exploded outward, knocking Sarah off her feet. She gasped, her body burning with a force she couldn’t understand. The shadows around them recoiled, as if they had been burned. The mansion itself seemed to shudder, its very foundation trembling under the weight of something ancient being released.  

And in that moment, Sarah saw it.  

Not just the mirror, but *everything*—visions of the past, of the mansion before it was a house, before it was a prison. She saw the mirror being created, the thing that had been locked inside it, the blood that had been spilled to keep it contained.  

And then, the moment snapped back into reality.  

Sarah gasped for air, her vision clearing as she found herself once again in the foyer, still holding Emily’s hand. The mirror was still there, but the shadows had receded, and the mansion was quiet once more.  

Emily looked at her, her face pale. “What did you see?”  

Sarah swallowed hard. “I saw it all,” she said. “The mansion, the mirror… the thing that was kept inside it. It was never meant to be sealed away. It was *meant* to be here.”  

Emily’s eyes widened. “Then we’re not just trespassers. We’re the *reason* it was sealed away.”  

Sarah’s breath caught in her throat. She didn’t know what that meant. But she knew one thing for certain.  

The mirror had awakened.  

And there was no turning back now.
